Non-Semantic HTML Tags:

  • div
    • A generic container with no meaning, often used for layout.

  • span

    • An inline container for styling or scripting purposes.

    Semantic HTML Tags:


  • header

    • Represets the introductory content or navigation links at the top of a page or section.

  • nav

    • Contains navigation links for easy site navigation.

  • aside

    • Represents side content related to the main content, such as sidebars or ads.

  • main

    • Defines the primary content of the webpage.

  • article

    • Represents self-contained content like blog posts or news articles.

  • figure

    • Used for images, diagrams, or other visual content, often paired with figcaption.

  • p

    • Defines a paragraph of text.

  • section

    • Groups related content within a page.

  • footer

    • Represents the footer of a webpage, containing copyright, links, or other metadata.

    Key Difference:

    1. Non-semantic tags (div, span) don't describe their purpose.
